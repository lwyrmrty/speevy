Summary

Harpoon Fund IV has committed to investing in Frontier Superintelligence alongside Lightspeed Venture Partners, which is leading the company's priced Seed-1 financing. Lightspeed will anchor the round with a $37.5 million investment, representing 75% of the total $50 million raise. Fund IV is investing on the same terms as Lightspeed. We are pleased to offer our limited partners the opportunity to co-invest alongside Harpoon Fund IV and Lightspeed at Seed in one of the most promising AI security companies in Silicon Valley.

In addition to the Fund's commitment, Harpoon has secured $3 million in co-investment allocation. The entry valuation for co-investors will be based on a fully diluted post-money valuation of $400 million.

Frontier Superintelligence is building an AI Operating System for enterprise Security Operations Centers. The platform connects security tools including SIEMs, EDR/NDR, identity management, ticketing, and threat intelligence through a single onboarding and orchestrates AI-driven workflows across the entire SOC operation. Core use cases include alert triage, detection rule engineering, incident response automation, and continuous red-teaming. Early deployments have demonstrated up to 80% time savings for frontline analysts. The product roadmap is structured in three phases, with the long-term vision of replacing fragmented point solutions with a unified Security Operating System.

The company is led by an exceptionally credentialed founding team. CEO Yaron Singer is a tenured Harvard professor in Computer Science and Applied Mathematics, a former researcher at Google AI and Microsoft AI, and the founder of Robust Intelligence, which was acquired by Cisco. Following the acquisition, Singer served as VP of AI and Engineering at Cisco, where he built Foundation AI, an open-source AI infrastructure research organization for cybersecurity. He is joined by Maya Bustan (VP Product), formerly SVP of Product at Snyk and Director of Product Management at Google, and Assaf Eisenman (VP Engineering), former Head of AI Engineering at Cisco and a five-year Technical Lead at Meta AI, where he led Llama training at scale. Together, the team brings rare depth across AI research, enterprise security product development, and large-scale infrastructure engineering. We view this as a high-conviction bet on a category-defining founder with whom we have built a relationship over several years.

Summary of Terms

This summary of terms describes the terms for investment in a special purpose entity (the “SPV”) organized by Harpoon Management, LLC (“Harpoon”) for the purpose of investing into Frontier Superintelligence, Inc. The SPV will be structured as a Delaware limited liability company. Harpoon will circulate an operating agreement and subscription agreement in respect of the SPV (collectively, the “Offering Documents”), which will set forth the terms of investment and risk factors, all of which are material to an investment in the SPV. Prior to investing, investors are strongly urged to carefully review the Offering Documents, and to discuss any prospective investment in the SPV with their legal and tax advisers in order to make an independent determination of the suitability and consequences of such an investment. This summary of terms is for discussion purposes only, and does not constitute an offer to sell or solicitation of an offer to buy any interests in the SPV. The final terms and conditions of an investment in the SPV shall only be set forth in definitive agreements with respect thereto.

Name of SPV:
Harpoon Ventures 2026-2, LLC
Investment:
The SPV’s sole purpose will be to invest in and hold securities of Frontier Superintelligence, Inc. (“Frontier”)
Manager:
Harpoon Ventures GP IV, LLC, a Delaware limited liability company affiliated with Harpoon.
The Offering:
Harpoon is seeking to secure approximately $2.50 million in aggregate capital commitments to the SPV. Harpoon intends to hold a single closing for all  investors in the SPV, expected in Q3 2026 (the “Closing”).
Term:
The  SPV will have an indefinite term. The Manager will seek favorable exit  opportunities for the SPV’s investment in Frontier, generally in coordination  with other Harpoon-managed investment vehicles with portfolio investments in Frontier. There is no predetermined schedule for liquidation of such investments.
Capital Contributions:
Capital commitments to the SPV will be called within 1 year of the closing.
Fees and Incentive Allocation:
The  SPV will not pay any direct management fees to the Manager. However, the Manager will have an incentive allocation pursuant to which 15% of distributions made by the SPV following the return of all capital contributions will be made to  the Manager.
Expenses:
The  SPV will be responsible for its costs and expenses, including (i) formation  and offering expenses, (ii) legal, accounting and administrative expenses  associated with the maintenance of the SPV and its ongoing operation, (iii) taxes, fees or other governmental charges applicable to the SPV, and (iv) expenses associated with the eventual liquidation of the SPV.
Withdrawal and Transfer:
Investors  may not voluntarily withdraw from the SPV, and may not sell, transfer or assign their interests in the SPV except with the consent of the Manager.
